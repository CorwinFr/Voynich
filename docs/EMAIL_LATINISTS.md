# Outreach message — Medieval Latin pharmaceutical experts

## Short version (for email / academic contact form / Twitter/X DM)

Subject: Request for expert feedback on computationally decoded Voynich Manuscript recipe lines

Dear Professor [Name],

I hope this message finds you well. I am writing to you as a specialist in medieval Latin pharmacy / Salernitan medical texts, and I would greatly appreciate your expert opinion on a small set of decoded text lines from the Voynich Manuscript (Beinecke MS 408).

Through computational analysis of the EVA transcription, we have identified a structural grammar that treats the manuscript as a pharmaceutical notation system rather than a cipher. When applied to the text, it produces recipe-like sequences involving oil (oleum), wax (cera), salt (sal), water (aqua), vinegar (acetum), and procedural verbs (misce, cola, adde), with quantities in drachms.

We do NOT claim to have "solved" the Voynich Manuscript. We are claiming only that the structural patterns we observe are statistically robust (e.g., raw/processed state markers correlate at r=0.956 across all manuscript sections) and that the decoded output resembles pharmaceutical Latin of the Antidotarium Nicolai tradition.

What we need is simple: would you be willing to look at 10-20 decoded recipe lines and tell us whether they read as plausible pharmaceutical instructions? We are equally interested in hearing what is wrong as what is right. A candid "this is nonsense" would be just as valuable as a confirmation.

I have prepared a short validation document (3 pages) with the decoded lines, our model summary, and specific questions. I would be happy to send it at your convenience.

Thank you very much for your time. I understand you receive many such requests regarding the Voynich Manuscript, and I appreciate any attention you can spare.

With warm regards,

## Tone guidelines

- HUMBLE: "we believe", "our analysis suggests", "we would value your expert opinion"
- HONEST: state the unknowns (20% undecoded, substance IDs are probable not proven)
- SPECIFIC: give them exactly 10-20 lines to read, not the whole manuscript
- RESPECTFUL of their time: offer the short document, don't dump 226 folios
- NO HYPE: absolutely no "we cracked the Voynich" language
- GRATEFUL: they are doing us a favor, not the other way around
