# Deep Research Prompt 2 : Traitements galéniques par signe zodiacal

## Context
We are studying the Voynich Manuscript (MS 408, 1404-1438) and have found that
the zodiac section pages have DIFFERENT pharmaceutical ingredient profiles for
each zodiac sign. This matches the medieval "zodiac man" (homo signorum) where
each sign governs a body part. Our computational analysis shows:

- Taurus (throat): VINEGAR dominant (6.8%) — suggesting gargles
- Gemini (arms): SALT + WATER dominant (9.1% each) — suggesting washes
- Cancer (chest/stomach): WATER dominant (8.7%) — suggesting oral potions
- Leo (heart): SALT dominant (8.5%) — suggesting mineral preparations
- Virgo (intestines): OIL dominant (5.1%) — suggesting oily laxatives
- Aquarius (legs): VINEGAR dominant (9.5%) — suggesting compresses

We need to verify: did medieval Galenic medicine actually prescribe these
specific substances for these specific body parts?
