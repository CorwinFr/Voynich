# Deep Research Prompt 3 : Calendriers lunaires de prescription

## Context
The Voynich Manuscript (MS 408, 1404-1438) contains a "volvelle" on folio f57v:
a rotating disk with 29 text positions around its circumference, corresponding
to the 29.5-day synodic lunar month. We believe this is a pharmaceutical
prescription calendar: the apothecary rotates the disk to today's lunar day
and reads which type of preparation is appropriate.

The text on the volvelle ring includes pharmaceutical vocabulary: SAL (salt),
AQUA (water), MEL (honey), quantities (2℥ = 2 ounces), and the verb
"Rx" (recipe/take).

We need to find REAL medieval lunar prescription calendars to compare.

## Questions

### 1. Did pharmaceutical lunar calendars exist?
- Were there medieval manuscripts where prescriptions were organized by lunar day?
- Did apothecaries use volvelles or rotating disks to determine daily treatments?
- Is there a documented tradition of "lunaria" (lunar medicine) in 15th-century Italy?
- How did the "critical days" theory (Galen) influence daily pharmaceutical practice?

### 2. Lunar days and medical practice
- Were specific lunar days associated with specific treatments?
- On which lunar days was bloodletting recommended vs forbidden?
- Were purgatives (laxatives) restricted to certain lunar phases?
- Did the type of medication change with the moon phase (waxing = tonics, waning = purgatives)?
- Were specific SUBSTANCES (salt, vinegar, oil, honey) associated with specific lunar days?

### 3. Volvelles in medical manuscripts
- Find examples of MEDICAL volvelles from the 14th-15th century
- MS Ashmole 370 has pharmaceutical content — does it have a volvelle?
- Peter of Abano (Padua, ~1300) wrote on medical astrology — did he describe lunar prescription systems?
- The Fasciculus Medicinae (1491) — does it contain lunar tables?
- Any volvelle with 29 or 30 positions arranged in a circle with text labels

### 4. The "Egyptian Days" and lunar medicine
- What are the "dies aegyptiaci" (Egyptian days) in medieval medicine?
- Were they linked to pharmaceutical preparation?
- How many "favorable" vs "unfavorable" days per lunar month?

### 5. Regimen Sanitatis and daily prescriptions
- The Regimen Sanitatis Salernitanum — does it organize advice by lunar day?
- Were there "almanacs" that told apothecaries what to prepare each day?
- The Tacuinum Sanitatis — does it include lunar timing for preparations?

### 6. Northern Italian pharmaceutical astronomy
- In the Veneto region (Padua, Venice) in the 15th century, what role did astrology play in pharmacy?
- Were Paduan medical faculty members (like Peter of Abano) still influential?
- Were there pharmacy guild regulations about lunar timing of preparations?

### 7. Sources to search
- Volvelles and medical manuscripts (medieval and early Renaissance)
- Peter of Abano, Conciliator Differentiarum
- Tacuinum Sanitatis (14th-15th century, multiple MSS)
- Fasciculus Medicinae (1491, Venice)
- MS Ashmole 370 (Oxford, Bodleian)
- Any "lunarium" or "liber lunae" medical text
- Studies by Nicolas Weill-Parot on medieval medical astrology
- Studies by Hilary Carey on medieval astrological medicine
- Charles Burnett on medieval science manuscripts

## Output format
For each source found:
1. Manuscript/text name and date
2. Does it organize treatments by lunar day?
3. Does it use a volvelle or circular diagram?
4. What substances/treatments are associated with which lunar phases?
5. Geographic origin (especially if Northern Italian)
6. Source reference / where digitized
